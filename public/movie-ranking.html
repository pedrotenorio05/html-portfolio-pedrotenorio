The Best Movies of all Time

By Pedro Tenório

Here is a list of my top 3 favorite movies of all time so far. Hope you enjoy!

1. Dune: Part II

Dune: Part II has quickly become my favorite movie, not only because I’m deeply in love with the Dune universe, but also because it brings to life my favorite book in such a powerful way. Denis Villeneuve has managed to capture the essence of Frank Herbert’s masterpiece, translating its complexity, depth, and grandeur onto the screen with breathtaking visuals and storytelling.

What I admire most about this adaptation is how it balances epic scale with intimate moments. The film doesn’t just recreate the story—it immerses the audience in it, making every scene feel both monumental and deeply personal. The performances, atmosphere, and world-building come together to honor the original work while giving it new life for modern audiences.

For me, Dune: Part II is more than just a movie; it’s an experience. It’s the kind of adaptation that makes you fall in love with the story all over again, reminding you why you cared about it in the first place. Villeneuve’s vision proves that great stories can transcend time and medium, leaving us with something unforgettable.

2. Interestellar



Interstellar stands out as a modern classic, seamlessly merging hard science with profound human emotion. Christopher Nolan crafts a narrative that is both intellectually ambitious and deeply moving, using the vastness of space as a backdrop to explore timeless themes of love, survival, and the resilience of the human spirit. It’s a film that challenges the mind while resonating with the heart, making it an essential work in contemporary cinema.

3. The Devil Wears Prada



The Devil Wears Prada is more than a stylish comedy-drama—it’s a sharp exploration of ambition, power, and identity within the high-pressure world of fashion. Anchored by Meryl Streep’s iconic performance as Miranda Priestly, the film cleverly examines the tension between personal values and professional success. Its blend of wit, elegance, and cultural critique has secured it a lasting place as both a crowd favorite and a commentary on modern career life.
